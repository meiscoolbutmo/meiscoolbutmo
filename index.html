What can a planet be?

People are often under the assumption that the IAU's definition of a planet is the objectivley correct definition and the only definition that exists or is true. I'm here to tell you that mindset is completely false. Sure, the IAU definition that demoted Pluto and dropped the official count to 8 is "official", but that doesn't make it universal.

I am going to pitch my idea for waht a planet should be here. It's designed to make large, geologically interesting objects planets while still seperating 'moon' from 'planet'.

Logan's Amazing Solar System Criteria

The L.A.S.S.C is a set of criteria I have put together to classify the Solar System. It divides into the four major categories: Asteroids, Worlds, Planets, and Stars, along with the more overlapping categories of Moons and Sattelites. By no means is the L.A.S.S.C official in any way, it is simply how I think space should be defined.

A star is the easiest to define, under these rules, a star is a object currently fusing elements due to the pressure of gravity. The Sun is thus the only star in the Solar System.

A world, in the context of the Solar System, is any object large enough to be mostly roundish due to its own gravity and/or differentaited into layers, alongside being too small to fuse any elements.

A planet, however, is more exclusive. It first must be a world, and if geologically active in some way, it must be greater than 1,000 km in diameter, and if geologically dead or uninteresting (essentially a boring rock), it mustbe greater than 2,000 km in diameter. A planet must also orbit a star, orbit nothing, or be in a binary with another planet. Binary systems are defined here as two bodies that orbit eachother with a mass ratio of greater than 1:100. The exact number of planets is uncertain due to the mysterious nature of the Kupiter Belt, but according to these criteria, the Solar System has at least 10-12 planets, although settling on 15 isn't a stretch, those 15 being Mercury, Venus, Terra (the Earth), Luna (the Moon), Mars, Jupier, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto, Haumea, Quaoar, Makemake, Gonggong, and Eris.

An asteroid, in the context of the Solar system, is anything that doesn't fit into these categories. Natural Sattelites are objects too small to be in a binary that also orbit either an asteroid, world, or planet. A moon is the same thing but reserved to just orbit planets.
